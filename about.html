Our Founder

His Highness Sri Chamarajendra Wodeyar Bahadur, the 23rd Ruler of the Yadu Dynasty, established Mysuru Zoo in 1892.

Designed to educate people about wildlife, not just for royal amusement.
Emphasis on comfort and well-being of animals.

In commemoration of esteemed founder, a bust of His Highness Sri Chamarajendra Wodeyar was installed near the entrance of zoo during the Centenary Celebration.

History & Growth

Mysuru Zoo has a rich history of over 128 years. Here are some key milestones in the growth of the zoo:

1892 Established with 10.9 acres area
1907 An additional 6.22 acres was included. For the first time large-ape enclosures were built in the country at Mysuru Zoo
1909 Zoo was named after the founder Sri Chamarajendra Wodeyar Bahadur. Mr. G.H. Krumbeigal, a German landscaper and horticulturist, created the beautiful landscape of Mysuru Zoo
1948 Administration of Mysuru Zoo transferred to Horticulture Department. Another 50 acres of Kensington Gardens were acquired, increasing total area to 78 acres
1960 Indian Board for Wildlife recommended designating Mysuru Zoo as a National Breeding Centre of wild animals with 100% financial assistance
1972 Administration of Mysuru Zoo was transferred to the Forest Department
1976 Mysuru Zoo acquired Karanji Lake
1979 Administration transferred to the Zoo Authority of Karnataka
1984 Launched the Animal Adoption Scheme. Koorghalli Rescue was started
2020 Mysuru Zoo has 128 years of history, modernized without shifting from the original place. Houses more than 1450 specimens from 168 species, representing 30+ countries
2022 Showcases a diverse collection of animals with a focus on conservation and education. Naturalistic enclosures aid successful breeding programs for endangered species, serving as a hub for wildlife awareness
