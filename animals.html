Our Family Members

Mysuru Zoo is home to a wide variety of animals from around the world. Explore our animal family and learn more about them.

Herbivores

Asiatic Elephant

The Asiatic Elephant is a native to the Indian subcontinent. Known for their intelligence and strong social bonds.

Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, recognized for their long necks and spotted patterns.

Hippopotamus

The Hippopotamus is a large, mostly herbivorous mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa.

African Elephant

The African Elephant is the largest land mammal, known for its intelligence and strong social bonds.

Barasingha Swamp Deer

Barasingha Swamp Deer are graceful creatures native to the wetlands of India, known for their stunning antlers.

Black Buck

Black Bucks are agile antelopes native to the Indian subcontinent, recognized by their striking black and white markings.

Cape Buffalo

The Cape Buffalo is a powerful bovine found in Africa, known for its formidable horns and strong herd instincts.

Four Horned Antelope

Four Horned Antelopes are unique creatures with four distinct horns, inhabiting the forests of India.

Goral

Gorals are small ungulates found in mountainous regions, known for their agility and tufted tails.

Guar

Guars are large antelopes native to the Indian subcontinent, recognized by their long, spiraling horns.

Hog Deer

Hog Deer are slender deer found in forested areas, known for their distinctive bristly tresses.

Indian Muntjac

Indian Muntjacs, also known as barking deer, are small ungulates with a distinctive vocalization.

Manipur Deer

Manipur Deer are graceful animals native to the northeastern regions, adapted to dense forests.

Nilgai

Nilgai, the largest antelope in India, are known for their impressive size and bluish-gray coat.

One Horned Rhino

The One Horned Rhino is a majestic creature, recognized by its single prominent horn and armored skin.

Sambar

Sambar deer are large, thick-built deer native to the Indian subcontinent, known for their robust antlers.

Southern White Rhino

The Southern White Rhino is one of the largest rhino species, distinguished by its wide mouth adapted for grazing.

Spotted Deer

Spotted Deer, also known as chital, are agile grazers with distinctive white spots on their reddish coats.

Swamp Deer

Swamp Deer are specially adapted to wetland habitats, with long legs and antlers suited for marshy terrains.

Tapir

Tapirs are large herbivorous mammals with distinctive short, prehensile snouts, inhabiting forested regions.

Wallaby

Wallabies are small to medium-sized marsupials similar to kangaroos, known for their agility and hopping ability.

Zebra

Zebras are iconic African equids known for their distinctive black and white striped coats.

Zebra

Zebras are known for their unique stripe patterns, which help in camouflage and social interactions.

Carnivores

Asiatic Lion

The Asiatic Lion is a panthera leo population surviving today only in India.

Bengal Tiger

Bengal Tigers are majestic big cats known for their power and strength.

Jaguar

The Jaguar is the largest cat species in the Americas and third largest in the world.

African Hunting Cheetah

The African Hunting Cheetah is renowned for its incredible speed and agility, making it the fastest land animal.

Bengal Fox

The Bengal Fox is a small canid native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its adaptability and keen hunting skills.

Dhole

Dholes are highly social wild dogs native to Asia, recognized for their cooperative hunting strategies.

Indian Grey Wolf

The Indian Grey Wolf is a resilient predator found in various habitats across India, known for its intelligence and pack behavior.

Jackal

Jackals are opportunistic omnivores known for their adaptability and cunning nature in the wild.

Jungle Cat

The Jungle Cat is a solitary feline species found in South and Southeast Asia, admired for its elusive behavior.

Leopard Cat

Leopard Cats are small wild cats native to Asia, known for their striking spotted coats and agility.

Leopard

Leopards are powerful big cats celebrated for their beautiful spotted fur and adaptability to diverse environments.

Otter

Otters are playful aquatic mammals known for their dexterity and social behavior in riverine habitats.

Slender Tailed Meerkat

Slender Tailed Meerkats are social creatures known for their vigilant behavior and cooperative living in the wild.

Smooth Coated Otter

The Smooth Coated Otter is distinguished by its sleek fur and is commonly found along the waterways of South Asia.

Striped Hyena

Striped Hyenas are nocturnal predators known for their scavenging habits and distinctive striped coats.

Wolf

Wolves are intelligent and social pack animals renowned for their strong communication and hunting prowess.

Lion

Lions are majestic big cats known as the "king of the jungle," recognized for their impressive mane and social structures.

White Tiger

White Tigers are a rare color variant of the Bengal Tiger, admired for their striking white coat and piercing eyes.

Birds

Blue and Gold Macaw

This colorful parrot is known for its vibrant blue and yellow plumage and social nature.

Great Indian Hornbill

A large bird featuring a distinctive casque on its bill, native to the forests of India.

White Peacock

The White Peacock is a rare variety known for its stunning white plumage.

Primates

Chimpanzee

Chimpanzees are highly intelligent primates closely related to humans.

Orangutan

Orangutans are great apes known for their reddish-brown hair and arboreal lifestyle.

Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest living primates and share much of their DNA with humans.

Brown Capuchin

Brown Capuchins are agile and intelligent primates known for their social behavior and dexterous hands.

Hamadryas Baboon

Hamadryas Baboons are distinctive primates with striking facial features and complex social structures.

Hanuman Langur

Hanuman Langurs are graceful primates recognized for their long tails and gentle demeanor.

Lemur

Lemurs are unique primates native to Madagascar, known for their large eyes and bushy tails.

Lion Tailed Macaque

Lion Tailed Macaques are endemic to the Western Ghats, recognized by their distinctive mane and agile movements.

Marmoset

Marmosets are small primates known for their lively spirits and intricate social interactions.

Nilgiri Labgur

Nilgiri Labgurs are adapted to the hilly terrains, showcasing remarkable climbing abilities and social structures.

Rhesus Macaque

Rhesus Macaques are versatile primates found in diverse habitats, known for their adaptability and intelligence.

Western Hoolock Gibbon

Western Hoolock Gibbons are masterful brachiators, famous for their melodious calls and strong family bonds.

Reptiles

King Cobra

The King Cobra is the world's longest venomous snake, feared and respected.

Green Iguana

A large, arboreal lizard known for its vibrant green color and dewlap.

Saltwater Crocodile

The largest of all living reptiles, found throughout the eastern Indo-Pacific region.

African Slender Snouted Crocodile

A rare crocodile species native to West Africa, known for its long, narrow snout and excellent swimming abilities.

Gharial

Gharials are distinguished by their long, slender snouts, native to the Indian subcontinent, primarily inhabiting rivers.

Green Vine Snake

An arboreal snake known for its vibrant green color and slender body, adept at navigating through dense foliage.

Indian Cobra

One of the most venomous snakes in India, recognized by its hood and distinctive threat display.

Malabar Pitviper

A venomous snake endemic to the Western Ghats, known for its potent venom and distinctive coloration.

Monitor Lizard

Large, intelligent reptiles known for their strong limbs and versatile hunting skills in diverse habitats.

Morelets Crocodile

A freshwater crocodile species found in Southeast Asia, recognized by its moderate size and robust build.

Reticulated Python

One of the world's longest snakes, known for its intricate pattern and powerful constricting abilities.

Siamese Crocodile

A critically endangered crocodile species native to Southeast Asia, recognized by its relatively small size and distinct markings.

Spectacled Caiman

Named for the bony ridge between their eyes, these caimans are common in freshwater habitats across South America.

Omnivores

Sloth Bear

Native to the Indian subcontinent, known for their shaggy coat and love for honey.

Asiatic Bear

Also known as the moon bear, recognized by the white crescent moon shape on their chest.
